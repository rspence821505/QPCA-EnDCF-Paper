\subsection{Incorrect Factorization of Joint Expectation in Bias Bound}
\label{issue:bias_factor}
\severity{L}

\noindent\textbf{Status:} Presentation/justification issue (the bound is valid via Jensen + Cauchy--Schwarz, but the intermediate steps are not shown).

\noindent\textbf{Location:} Lines 1305--1310 (proof of Theorem~4, bias bound).

\noindent\textbf{Statement in paper:}
\[
\left\|\mathbb{E}\!\left[\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}
(\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}-\mathbf{P}_\kappa^{\,s})\,\bm{\mu}_E^{\,s}\right]\right\|^2
\;\le\;
\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2^2\;\|\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\|_2\;
\mathbb{E}\|\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}-\mathbf{P}_\kappa^{\,s}\|_F^2\;
\|\bm{\mu}_E^{\,s}\|^2.
\]

\noindent\textbf{Issue:}
As written, the paper jumps directly from $\|\mathbb{E}[\cdot]\|^2$ to a product of expectations, which can be misread as an (incorrect) independence-based factorization. In fact, the displayed bound is obtainable without any independence assumption by combining (i) Jensen's inequality $\|\mathbb{E}[Z]\|^2 \le (\mathbb{E}\|Z\|)^2$ with (ii) Cauchy--Schwarz applied to $\mathbb{E}[\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2\,\|\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}-\mathbf{P}_\kappa^{\,s}\|_F]$.

\noindent\textbf{Correct approach (one-line justification):}
Let $Z=\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}(\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}-\mathbf{P}_\kappa^{\,s})\bm{\mu}_E^{\,s}$. Then
\begin{align*}
\|\mathbb{E}[Z]\|^2
&\le (\mathbb{E}\|Z\|)^2 \\
&\le \|\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\|_2\|\bm{\mu}_E^{\,s}\|^2
\bigl(\mathbb{E}[\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2\,\|\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}-\mathbf{P}_\kappa^{\,s}\|_F]\bigr)^2 \\
&\le \|\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\|_2\|\bm{\mu}_E^{\,s}\|^2\,
\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2^2\,
\mathbb{E}\|\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}-\mathbf{P}_\kappa^{\,s}\|_F^2.
\end{align*}

\noindent\textbf{Recommended fix:}
In \texttt{uq\_paper\_draft.tex} around lines 1305--1311, insert a brief justification (one or two displayed lines) indicating that the bound follows from Jensen's inequality and Cauchy--Schwarz (as above). This makes clear that no independence between $\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}$ and $\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}$ is being assumed.

\subsection{Dropped Term in the Bias Decomposition}
\label{issue:dropped_term}
\severity{H}

\noindent\textbf{Status:} Valid proof gap unless an additional independence argument is invoked (e.g., Gaussian mean/covariance independence).

\noindent\textbf{Location:} Lines 1287--1296 (proof of Theorem~4).

\noindent\textbf{Statement in paper:}
The proof decomposes $\mathbb{E}[\bar{\mathbf{x}}^{a,s}]$ by inserting and subtracting $(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}\mathbf{P}_\kappa^s\bm{\mu}_E^s$:
\[
\mathbb{E}[\bar{\mathbf{x}}^{a,s}]
= \bm{\mu}^f
- \mathbb{E}[\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}\mathbf{P}_\kappa^s\,\bm{\mu}_E^s]
- \mathbb{E}[\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}(\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^s - \mathbf{P}_\kappa^s)\,\bm{\mu}_E^s].
\]

\noindent\textbf{Issue:}
The correct algebraic decomposition of $\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa \bar{\mathbf{e}}^s$ is:
\[
\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa\,\bar{\mathbf{e}}^s
= \mathbf{P}_\kappa\,\bm{\mu}_E^s
+ (\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa - \mathbf{P}_\kappa)\,\bm{\mu}_E^s
+ \hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa\,(\bar{\mathbf{e}}^s - \bm{\mu}_E^s).
\]
After multiplying by $\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}$ and taking expectations, the full expression is:
\begin{align*}
\mathbb{E}\!\left[\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}\bar{\mathbf{e}}^{\,s}\right]
&=
\mathbb{E}\!\left[\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}\mathbf{P}_\kappa^{\,s}\boldsymbol{\mu}_E^{\,s}\right]
\;+\;
\mathbb{E}\!\left[\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}(\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s} - \mathbf{P}_\kappa^{\,s})\boldsymbol{\mu}_E^{\,s}\right] \\
&\quad +\;
\mathbb{E}\!\left[\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}(\bar{\mathbf{e}}^{\,s}-\boldsymbol{\mu}_E^{\,s})\right].
\end{align*}
The third term
\(
\mathbb{E}[\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}(\bar{\mathbf{e}}^{\,s}-\boldsymbol{\mu}_E^{\,s})]
\)
is dropped without justification. While $\mathbb{E}[\bar{\mathbf{e}}^{\,s}-\boldsymbol{\mu}_E^{\,s}] = 0$, the quantities $\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}$, $\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}$, and $(\bar{\mathbf{e}}^{\,s}-\boldsymbol{\mu}_E^{\,s})$ are computed from the same ensemble, so the expectation of their product does \emph{not} factor in general.

\noindent\textbf{When it vanishes:}
If the ensemble residuals are Gaussian and the gain/projector are functions only of centered anomalies (so that $(\mathbf{K},\hat{\mathbf{P}})$ is measurable with respect to the centered residual matrix $\mathbf{E}_c$), then the Gaussian independence of sample mean and sample covariance implies $(\bar{\mathbf{e}}-\bm{\mu})$ is independent of $(\mathbf{K},\hat{\mathbf{P}})$. In that case,
\(
\mathbb{E}[\mathbf{K}\mathbf{R}^{1/2}\hat{\mathbf{P}}(\bar{\mathbf{e}}-\bm{\mu})]=\mathbf{0}.
\)
Absent such an assumption, the term need not vanish.

\noindent\textbf{Recommended fix:}
In \texttt{uq\_paper\_draft.tex} around lines 1287--1296, explicitly add and bound the missing term
\[
\mathbb{E}\!\left[\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}\,(\bar{\mathbf{e}}^{\,s}-\boldsymbol{\mu}_E^{\,s})\right]
\]
in one of the following ways:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \emph{Assumption route:} state a Gaussian/independence hypothesis at this step (or as an additional assumption in the theorem) under which $(\bar{\mathbf{e}}^{\,s}-\boldsymbol{\mu}_E^{\,s})$ is independent of $(\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}},\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s})$, so the term is exactly zero.
\item \emph{Bound route (no independence):} use $\|\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}\|_2\le 1$ and Cauchy--Schwarz to obtain
\[
\left\|\mathbb{E}\!\left[\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^{1/2}\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}\,(\bar{\mathbf{e}}^{\,s}-\boldsymbol{\mu}_E^{\,s})\right]\right\|^2
\le
\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2^2\,\|\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\|_2\,\mathbb{E}\|\bar{\mathbf{e}}^{\,s}-\boldsymbol{\mu}_E^{\,s}\|^2
=
\frac{\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E^{\,s})}{N}\,\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2^2\,\|\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\|_2.
\]
\end{itemize}
This yields an additional finite-$N$ bias contribution of order $N^{-1}$ that can be included explicitly in the bias bound (or absorbed into existing constants if the bias bound is only intended up to $O(N^{-1})$ terms).

\subsection{Unresolved Joint Expectation in Variance Bound}
\label{issue:variance_joint}
\severity{M}

\noindent\textbf{Status:} Partially valid (the variance bound is fine; the ``In particular'' refinement does not directly apply to the joint term as written).

\noindent\textbf{Location:} Lines 1244--1246 (variance bound) vs.\ lines 1250--1261 (``In particular'' refinement).

\noindent\textbf{Statement in paper:}
The variance bound (eq.\ at line 1247) contains:
\[
2\|\mathbf{R}\|_2\;\mathbb{E}\bigl[\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2^2\,
\|(\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa - \mathbf{P}_\kappa)(\bar{\mathbf{e}} - \bm{\mu})\|^2\bigr].
\]
The subsequent refinement (eqs.\ 1250--1261) bounds only the marginal:
\[
\mathbb{E}\bigl[\|(\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa - \mathbf{P}_\kappa)(\bar{\mathbf{e}} - \bm{\mu})\|^2\bigr]
\;\le\;
\frac{8\kappa}{\delta_\kappa^2}\,
\bigl(\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{C}_E - \bm{\Sigma}_E\|_F^4\bigr)^{1/2}
\bigl(\mathbb{E}\|\bar{\mathbf{e}} - \bm{\mu}\|^4\bigr)^{1/2}.
\]

\noindent\textbf{Issue:}
The text implies (via ``In particular'') that the marginal bound directly refines the third term of the variance bound, but the variance bound has the \emph{joint} expectation $\mathbb{E}[\|\mathbf{K}\|_2^2 \cdot Y^2]$, not $\mathbb{E}[\|\mathbf{K}\|_2^2] \cdot \mathbb{E}[Y^2]$. Since $\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}$ and $Y = \|(\hat{\mathbf{P}} - \mathbf{P})(\bar{\mathbf{e}} - \bm{\mu})\|$ are dependent, the substitution is not valid without an additional Cauchy--Schwarz step (which would require fourth moments of $\mathbf{K}$). The variance bound itself (eq.\ 1247) is correct as stated; the gap is in how the refinement relates to it.

\noindent\textbf{Note:} The proof on line 1329 correctly acknowledges that $\hat{\mathbf{P}}$ and $(\bar{\mathbf{e}} - \bm{\mu})$ are dependent and uses Cauchy--Schwarz on fourth moments for eq.\ (1260). However, the same care is \emph{not} taken for the $\|\mathbf{K}\|_2^2$ factor.

\noindent\textbf{Recommended fix:}
In \texttt{uq\_paper\_draft.tex} around lines 1248--1250, modify the ``In particular'' discussion so that it does not suggest the marginal projector bound directly refines the \emph{joint} term in the variance bound (eq.\ at lines 1232--1247 of the paper). Concretely, one of the following options resolves the issue:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \emph{Minimal (presentation) fix:} keep the variance bound as stated and change the lead-in ``In particular'' to a separate remark, e.g.\ ``Moreover, the projector factor admits the marginal bound (eq.\ at lines 1250--1261).'' Then make explicit that combining the joint term (lines 1244--1246) with the marginal bound requires additional control of $\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2$ beyond the second-moment assumption currently stated for the gain.
\item \emph{Uniform-bound route:} if the gain is computed using a stabilized inverse with an almost-sure bound $\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2\le K_{\max}$ (as is the case for Tikhonov/ridge with fixed regularization and bounded anomalies), then
\[
\mathbb{E}\!\left[\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2^2 Y^2\right]
\le
K_{\max}^2\,\mathbb{E}[Y^2],
\]
so the marginal projector bound (lines 1250--1261) can be used directly in the joint variance term (lines 1244--1246), with an extra prefactor $K_{\max}^2$.
\item \emph{Moment route:} strengthen the gain moment assumption (Assumption at lines 612--619 of \texttt{uq\_paper\_draft.tex}) to $\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2^{4}<\infty$ and insert the missing Hölder/Cauchy--Schwarz step
\[
\mathbb{E}\!\left[\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2^2 Y^2\right]
\le
\bigl(\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{K}^{\mathrm{DC}}\|_2^4\bigr)^{1/2}\,
\bigl(\mathbb{E}Y^4\bigr)^{1/2}.
\]
This makes the dependence on gain moments explicit; to close the bound fully one must then control $\mathbb{E}Y^4$ (e.g., via higher-moment bounds on $\hat{\mathbf{P}}_\kappa^{\,s}-\mathbf{P}_\kappa^{\,s}$ and $\bar{\mathbf{e}}^{\,s}-\boldsymbol{\mu}_E^{\,s}$).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Corollary~2 Covariance Identity Invalid for Random Gain}
\label{issue:corollary2}
\severity{M}

\noindent\textbf{Status:} Valid as stated for deterministic gain; for random gain the identity should be conditional or averaged over the gain randomness.

\noindent\textbf{Location:} Lines 1397, 1409--1413 (Corollary~2, stochastic comparison).

\noindent\textbf{Statement in paper:}
\[
\mathrm{Cov}(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^a_{\mathrm{stoch}})
= \mathrm{Cov}(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^a_{\mathrm{det}})
+ \frac{1}{N}\,\mathbf{K}^{(w)}\mathbf{R}^{(L)}(\mathbf{K}^{(w)})^\top,
\]
where $\mathbf{K}^{(w)}$ is ``deterministic, or more generally independent of $\{\bm{\epsilon}_w^{(j)}\}$'' (line~1397).

\noindent\textbf{Issue:}
When $\mathbf{K}^{(w)}$ is deterministic, the identity is correct and the additive $(1/N)\mathbf{K}\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\mathbf{K}^\top$ term is the covariance of the sample-mean perturbation $\frac{1}{N}\sum_j \mathbf{K}\bm{\epsilon}^{(j)}$.

When $\mathbf{K}^{(w)}$ is random (but independent of $\{\bm{\epsilon}^{(j)}\}$), the cross-covariance between $\bar{\mathbf{x}}^a_{\mathrm{det}}$ (which depends on the ensemble) and the perturbation term still vanishes because $\mathbb{E}[\bm{\epsilon}^{(j)}]=0$ and $\bm{\epsilon}^{(j)}$ is independent of the ensemble. The main issue is that the final term should not be written as $\frac{1}{N}\mathbf{K}\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\mathbf{K}^\top$ \emph{inside an unconditional covariance identity}; one must either condition on $\mathbf{K}$ (or the ensemble) or take expectation over $\mathbf{K}$.

By the law of total covariance:
\[
\mathrm{Cov}(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^a_{\mathrm{stoch}})
= \mathbb{E}[\mathrm{Cov}(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^a_{\mathrm{stoch}} \mid \mathbf{K})]
+ \mathrm{Cov}(\mathbb{E}[\bar{\mathbf{x}}^a_{\mathrm{stoch}} \mid \mathbf{K}]).
\]
The conditional decomposition gives
\[
\mathrm{Cov}(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^a_{\mathrm{stoch}} \mid \mathbf{K})
=
\mathrm{Cov}(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^a_{\mathrm{det}} \mid \mathbf{K})
+
\frac{1}{N}\,\mathbf{K}\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\mathbf{K}^\top,
\]
and taking expectations yields
\(
\mathrm{Cov}(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^a_{\mathrm{stoch}})
=
\mathrm{Cov}(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^a_{\mathrm{det}})
+
\frac{1}{N}\mathbb{E}[\mathbf{K}\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\mathbf{K}^\top].
\)

\noindent\textbf{Recommended fix:}
The clean fix is to decide whether Corollary~2 is meant to be conditional on the gain (and/or the forecast ensemble) or fully unconditional, and then rewrite the covariance identity accordingly:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \emph{Deterministic-gain route (minimal change):} in \texttt{uq\_paper\_draft.tex} line 1397, keep only ``$\mathbf{K}^{(w)}$ is deterministic'' and leave eq.\ at lines 1409--1413 unchanged.
\item \emph{Conditional-identity route (keeps random gain allowed):} in \texttt{uq\_paper\_draft.tex} replace eq.\ at lines 1409--1413 with a conditional statement, for example
\[
\mathrm{Cov}\!\left(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^{a}_{\mathrm{stoch}}\mid \mathbf{K}^{(w)}\right)
=
\mathrm{Cov}\!\left(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^{a}_{\mathrm{det}}\mid \mathbf{K}^{(w)}\right)
+
\frac{1}{N}\mathbf{K}^{(w)}\mathbf{R}^{(L)}(\mathbf{K}^{(w)})^\top,
\]
and add one sentence noting that taking expectations yields the unconditional additive term $\frac{1}{N}\mathbb{E}[\mathbf{K}^{(w)}\mathbf{R}^{(L)}(\mathbf{K}^{(w)})^\top]$.
\item \emph{Unconditional-identity route (explicit averaging over the random gain):} keep ``$\mathbf{K}^{(w)}$ independent of $\{\boldsymbol{\epsilon}_w^{(j)}\}$'' in line 1397, but replace eq.\ at lines 1409--1413 by
\[
\mathrm{Cov}\!\left(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^{a}_{\mathrm{stoch}}\right)
=
\mathrm{Cov}\!\left(\bar{\mathbf{x}}^{a}_{\mathrm{det}}\right)
+
\frac{1}{N}\,\mathbb{E}\!\left[\mathbf{K}^{(w)}\mathbf{R}^{(L)}(\mathbf{K}^{(w)})^\top\right].
\]
In this case, also update eq.\ at lines 1421--1426 so that
\(
\mathbb{E}\bigl\|\frac{1}{N}\sum_j \mathbf{K}^{(w)}\boldsymbol{\epsilon}_w^{(j)}\bigr\|^2
=
\frac{1}{N}\mathbb{E}\,\mathrm{tr}(\mathbf{K}^{(w)}\mathbf{R}^{(L)}(\mathbf{K}^{(w)})^\top)
\le
\frac{\mathrm{tr}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})}{N}\,\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{K}^{(w)}\|_2^2.
\)
\end{itemize}

\label{issue:variance_scaling}
\severity{M}

\noindent\textbf{Status:} Partially valid (requires additional eigenvalue scaling assumptions; otherwise should be phrased as $\mathcal{O}(1/N)$ with $\kappa$-dependent prefactors).

\noindent\textbf{Location:} Lines 1711 and 1765.

\noindent\textbf{Statement in paper:}
``For QPCA-EnDCF, theory shows variance scaling as $\mathcal{O}(\kappa/N)$, where $\kappa$ is the truncation level'' (line~1708).

\noindent\textbf{Issue:}
Theorem~4's variance bound (eq.\ at lines 1233--1247) is:
\[
\mathrm{Variance} \;\le\;
\frac{2}{N}\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{x}^{(1),f} - \bm{\mu}^f\|^2
+ \frac{2}{N}\,\mathbb{E}\!\left[\|\mathbf{K}\|_2^2\right]\,

The first two terms scale as $\mathcal{O}(1/N)$ and, as stated, they do \emph{not} exhibit an explicit $\kappa$ factor (the second term uses $\mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma}_E^s)$ rather than a truncated trace such as $\mathrm{tr}(\mathbf{P}_\kappa\bm{\Sigma}_E^s)$). Thus, the sentence ``variance scaling as $\mathcal{O}(\kappa/N)$'' is not literally supported by the stated theorem without an additional argument translating the observation-space variance contribution into an \emph{effective-dimension} quantity (e.g., replacing $\mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma}_E^s)$ by a truncated trace such as $\sum_{i=1}^{\kappa}\lambda_i^{\,s}$) and then assuming a normalization like $\lambda_i^{\,s}=\mathcal{O}(1)$.

\noindent\textbf{Recommended fix:}
In \texttt{uq\_paper\_draft.tex}, rewrite the two occurrences of ``$\mathcal{O}(\kappa/N)$'' to match what Theorem~4 actually provides:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \emph{Safe (fully consistent) wording:} replace the first sentence at line 1711 by
\begin{quote}
``For QPCA-EnDCF, Theorem~4 yields $\mathrm{Variance}=\mathcal{O}(1/N)$ with $\kappa$-dependent prefactors arising through projector stability (via $\kappa/\delta_\kappa^2$) and the dependence of the observation-space term on $\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E)$.''%
\end{quote}
and replace the clause at line 1765 by ``With variance scaling as $\mathcal{O}(1/N)$ (with $\kappa$-dependent prefactors) rather than $\mathcal{O}(r/N)$, \ldots''.
\item \emph{If you want an explicit $\kappa/N$ statement:} state the additional assumption needed (e.g., a bound $\mathrm{tr}(\mathbf{P}_\kappa\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E)\lesssim \kappa$ under eigenvalue normalization) and either (i) tighten the theorem's second variance term accordingly, or (ii) explicitly qualify the sentence as a heuristic/effective-dimension interpretation (``in regimes where $\sum_{i=1}^{\kappa}\lambda_i=\mathcal{O}(\kappa)$, the $\kappa$-sensitive contribution behaves like $\mathcal{O}(\kappa/N)$'').
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Frobenius Norm Scaling Claim}
\label{issue:frobenius}
\severity{M}

\noindent\textbf{Status:} Potentially incorrect scaling in $d$ unless the implied big-$\mathcal{O}$ constant is allowed to depend on dimension.

\noindent\textbf{Location:} Lines 187--206 (discussion of sampling error in $\widehat{\mathbf{R}}^{(L)}$) and in particular lines 197--205.

\noindent\textbf{Statement in paper:}
\[
\left(\mathbb{E}\bigl[\|\widehat{\mathbf{R}}^{(L)} - \mathbf{R}^{(L)}\|_F^2\bigr]\right)^{1/2}
= \mathcal{O}\!\left(\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}^2\sqrt{\frac{d}{N-1}}\right).
\]

\noindent\textbf{Issue:}
For $\mathbf{R}^{(L)} = \sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}^2\mathbf{I}_d$ (the diagonal case), the exact Wishart formula gives:
\[
\mathbb{E}\|\hat{\mathbf{R}}^{(L)} - \mathbf{R}^{(L)}\|_F^2
= \frac{\mathrm{tr}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^2 + \|\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\|_F^2 \cdot (\text{constant})}{N-1}
= \frac{d^2\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}^4 + d\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}^4}{N-1}.
\]
Taking the square root gives $\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}^2\sqrt{d(d+1)/(N{-}1)} = \mathcal{O}(\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}^2\,d/\sqrt{N})$, not $\mathcal{O}(\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}^2\sqrt{d/N})$. The stated bound is off by a factor of $\sqrt{d}$ relative to the exact Wishart answer.

However, the paper's own Lemma~3 gives a bound in terms of $\mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma}^2)/(N{-}1)$ with a dimension-dependent constant $C_{\mathrm{cov}}$, which may absorb the extra $\sqrt{d}$ factor. If the claim is meant as a general big-O statement (with constants depending on $d$), it should be stated as such.

\noindent\textbf{Recommended fix:}
In \texttt{uq\_paper\_draft.tex} at lines 197--205, make the scaling consistent with the Frobenius-norm identity you intend to invoke:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \emph{If the norm is truly Frobenius (recommended):} replace the display at lines 199--203 by the explicit Gaussian/Wishart expression (for $\mathbf{R}^{(L)}=\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}^2\mathbf{I}_d$)
\[
\left(\mathbb{E}\bigl[\|\widehat{\mathbf{R}}^{(L)}-\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\|_F^2\bigr]\right)^{1/2}
=
\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}^2\sqrt{\frac{d(d+1)}{N-1}}
=
\mathcal{O}\!\left(\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}^2\,\frac{d}{\sqrt{N}}\right),
\]
and update the concluding sentence (line 206) from ``the prefactor grows like $\sqrt{L}$'' to ``the prefactor grows like $L$'' (since $d=mL$). If you want to keep the discussion distribution-agnostic, the corresponding general-Gaussian formula is
\(
\left(\mathbb{E}\|\widehat{\mathbf{R}}^{(L)}-\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\|_F^2\right)^{1/2}
=
\mathcal{O}\!\left(\sqrt{\frac{\mathrm{tr}((\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^2)+\mathrm{tr}(\mathbf{R}^{(L)})^2}{N}}\right).
\)
\item \emph{If you intended the $\sqrt{d/N}$ scaling:} change the norm in the display and subsequent sentence to an operator-norm statement. For Gaussian $\mathbf{R}^{(L)}=\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}^2\mathbf{I}_d$, a spectral-norm bound has the form
\(
\left(\mathbb{E}\|\widehat{\mathbf{R}}^{(L)}-\mathbf{R}^{(L)}\|_2^2\right)^{1/2}

(up to the usual additional $d/N$ term in high dimension). This preserves the ``$\sqrt{L}$ amplification'' narrative but requires using $\|\cdot\|_2$ rather than $\|\cdot\|_F$ throughout that paragraph.

\subsection{Lemma~3: $\mathbb{E}[\|\tilde{\mathbf{e}}\|^4]$ Bound Unjustified}
\label{issue:fourth_moment}
\severity{M}

\noindent\textbf{Status:} Partially valid (a $\mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma}^2)$-only bound can hold with an explicit dimension factor; the missing step/assumptions should be made explicit).

\noindent\textbf{Location:} Lemma~3 (Mean-square Frobenius deviation of the sample covariance), especially the proof lines 868--876 and the optional Gaussian remark at lines 879--880.

\noindent\textbf{Issue:}
For the Gaussian case, $\mathbb{E}\|\tilde{\mathbf{e}}\|^4$ involves both $\mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma})^2$ and $\mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma}^2)$. However, since $\mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma})^2 \le d\,\mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma}^2)$, a bound of the form $C(d)\,\mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma}^2)$ can still be valid provided the dimension dependence is made explicit and justified.

As written, the paper states a $C(d)\,\mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma}^2)$ bound without derivation, and for non-Gaussian ensembles a fourth-moment (bounded-kurtosis) condition is typically required to control $\mathbb{E}\|\tilde{\mathbf{e}}\|^4$ with constants that do not blow up unexpectedly.

\noindent\textbf{Recommended fix:}
In \texttt{uq\_paper\_draft.tex} near the end of the Lemma~3 proof (lines 868--876), make explicit the step that reduces $\mathbb{E}\|\tilde{\mathbf{e}}^{(1)}\|^4$ to a multiple of $\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E^2)$, and state the corresponding assumption/constant dependence. Concretely:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \emph{Gaussian route (fully explicit):} add one displayed line (or sentence) using the exact identity
\[
\mathbb{E}\|\tilde{\mathbf{e}}^{(1)}\|^4
=
\bigl(\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E)\bigr)^2 + 2\,\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E^2)
\le
(d+2)\,\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E^2),
\]
where the last inequality uses $\bigl(\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E)\bigr)^2\le d\,\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E^2)$. This justifies the ``absorb into $C_{\mathrm{cov}}$'' sentence and clarifies the dimension dependence.
\item \emph{Non-Gaussian route (standardized kurtosis):} define an explicit ``standardized fourth moment'' parameter, e.g.
\[
\kappa_4:=\sup_{\|u\|_2=1}\frac{\mathbb{E}\bigl[(u^\top \tilde{\mathbf{e}}^{(1)})^4\bigr]}{\bigl(u^\top \boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E u\bigr)^2}<\infty,
\]
and then add the short bound (using the eigenbasis of $\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E$) showing
\(
\mathbb{E}\|\tilde{\mathbf{e}}^{(1)}\|^4 \le \kappa_4\,d\,\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E^2).
\)
Then state explicitly that $C_{\mathrm{cov}}$ depends on $\kappa_4$ and $d$ (not just on the existence of a finite fourth moment).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Gaussian Constant $C_{\mathrm{cov}} = 8(d+2)$ Needs Verification}
\label{issue:gaussian_const}
\severity{L}

\noindent\textbf{Status:} Needs verification (may be correct depending on how $C_{\mathrm{cov}}$ is defined).

\noindent\textbf{Location:} Lemma~3 (Mean-square Frobenius deviation of the sample covariance), especially the optional Gaussian remark at lines 879--880 of \texttt{uq\_paper\_draft.tex}.

\noindent\textbf{Issue:}
The constant $C_{\mathrm{cov}} = 8(d+2)$ is stated for the Gaussian case. Standard Wishart distribution results give:
\[
\mathbb{E}\|\hat{\bm{\Sigma}} - \bm{\Sigma}\|_F^2
= \frac{1}{N-1}\bigl[\mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma})^2 + \mathrm{tr}(\bm{\Sigma}^2)\bigr]
\quad\text{(for Gaussian data)}.
\]
Since Lemma~3 is stated in the form
\(
\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{C}_E-\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E\|_F^2 \le \frac{C_{\mathrm{cov}}}{N-1}\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E^2),
\)
the exact Gaussian identity implies an explicit admissible choice
\[
C_{\mathrm{cov}} = 1 + \frac{\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E)^2}{\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E^2)}
\le d+1,
\]
using $\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E)^2\le d\,\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E^2)$. Therefore $8(d+2)$ is a very conservative constant; it may still be correct as a bound, but the remark should either (i) present the exact identity, or (ii) explain how $8(d+2)$ arises from the particular inequality chain used in the proof.

\noindent\textbf{Recommended fix:}
In \texttt{uq\_paper\_draft.tex} lines 879--880, replace the current sentence with either an exact statement or an explicit bound consistent with Lemma~3:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \emph{Exact identity (preferred):} ``If $\tilde{\mathbf{e}}^{(1)}$ is Gaussian, then $(N-1)\mathbf{C}_E$ is Wishart and
\(
\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{C}_E-\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E\|_F^2=\frac{\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E)^2+\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E^2)}{N-1}.
\)''
\item \emph{Explicit constant in Lemma~3 form:} append ``In particular, $\mathbb{E}\|\mathbf{C}_E-\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E\|_F^2 \le \frac{d+1}{N-1}\mathrm{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_E^2)$.'' If you keep $8(d+2)$, add a brief derivation or state explicitly that it is a loose but convenient choice coming from the proof inequalities.
\end{itemize}
